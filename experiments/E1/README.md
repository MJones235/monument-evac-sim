# E1 — Fire alarm only

## Scenario

The fire alarm sounds at t=15s. No staff are present, and no public address announcement is made.
Passengers must decide how to respond based solely on hearing the alarm.

## Hypothesis

Without additional information or direction, passengers will exhibit a range of behaviours:
hesitation, information-seeking, following others, and delayed evacuation. A significant
proportion will remain in the station for an extended period before evacuating.

## Key metrics

- Time for 50% / 90% / 100% of agents to exit
- Proportion of agents who immediately move toward an exit vs. wait/hesitate
- Distribution of exit used (concourse exits vs. other)
- Number of agents still on the platform at t=60s, t=90s, t=120s

## Notes

This is the **control condition**. All other experiments (E2–E5) are compared against E1.
